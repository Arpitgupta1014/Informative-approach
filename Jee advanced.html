What is JEE Advanced Exam & How to Prepare for it?

 

IIT JEE Advanced Exam is India’s toughest Engineering Entrance Exam. It is held by one of the IITs in the month of May every year.

Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is conducted in two phases:

   1. JEE Mains

   2. JEE Advanced

JEE Advanced Exam is the later stage of the Two-phase nationwide entrance exam for admission in Bachelor of Engineering Courses in IITs and ISM Dhanbad.

Needless to mention, IITs are famous & topmost technical education institutes in India. These pioneer 23 institutes select brightest science students against approximately 11000 engineering undergraduate seats.

IIT JEE Advanced Exam is preceded by JEE Mains Examination. Candidates qualifying JEE Mains are considered eligible to appear for JEE Advanced Exam.

Are you Eligible for JEE Advanced?

As per the new rules of JEE Advanced, Aadhar card will be a compulsory document for JEE exam applicants

Any candidate failing to produce the Aadhar card will not be considered eligible for JEE Advanced Exam.

The detailed eligibility criteria is prescribed on the official JEE Advanced Portal.

Candidates are suggested to read the eligibility criteria before applying for the exam.

It can be classified in following five factors:

  1. Appearance in Class XII
  2. Candidate’s Performance in JEE Mains
  3. Age Limit
  4. Number of Attempts
  5. Earlier Admission in IITs

Criteria of top 20 percentile for JEE Advanced 2021: For admission in IITs & ISM, one must fulfil the Criteria of top 20 percentile in board exams.

The examination is open for Foreign & Indian nationals.JEE Advanced Eligibility criteria will be uploaded soon.

How to Apply for JEE Advanced Exam?

A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step. Your first step towards IIT JEE Advanced Exam is the Online Application Process.

JEE Advanced Exam for 2021 session will be on 3rd July 2020. You must be very careful & attentive while filling the JEE Advanced Form. So, if you fulfil the eligibility conditions, then fill the application form very carefully.

Instructions to Candidates for Filling JEE Advanced Application Form

A candidate must have his/her photograph and signature duly scanned in the jpg format in such a manner that image size should be between 50 KB to 300 KB for the photograph and 1 KB for the signature.

Candidate’s Personal Contact Number & Email ID are the pre-requisites.

If you belong to the reserved category keep caste certificate handy. Several times, students claim they belong to a particular caste, which is considered as OBC by his or her state but not by the Centre. Such candidates are rejected. Make sure your described details match with the guidelines.

Review the Application form thoroughly before submitting it.

Once you’ve successfully submitted the JEE Advanced Application Form, Save the JOSAA Candidate login details & write it in a notepad. You’ll need the JEE Advanced login in future.

What is the JEE Advanced Paper Pattern - Paper 1, Paper 2 & AAT

JEE Advanced Paper Pattern is the most important thing that will carve your preparation plan. JEE convener institutes keep on changing the exam pattern & marking scheme frequently.

The paper can be substituted in the following parts:

  1. JEE Advanced 2021 Paper 1 - Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics.
  2. JEE Advanced 2021 Paper 2 - Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics.
  3. AAT (for Architecture) - Free hand drawing, Geometrical drawing, 3D perception, Imagination & Aesthetic Sensitivity, Architectural awareness.

Have a look at the JEE Advanced 2021 Pattern, to know about the type of questions, marking scheme etc.

Paper 1 and Paper 2

 The question papers will contain MCQ type and Numerical Answer type questions.
JEE Advanced Paper 1 & 2 will have three section and exam duration of three hours for each.
Total Marks: 372.
There is negative marking in the exam. It discourages random guesses in MCQs, and therefore the scores are more reliable. Negative marking is also varies year to year.

Architecture Aptitude Test:

JEE Architecture(AAT) Exam will be held through offline mode only.
There are 5 sections in the paper. Overall exam duration will be 3 hours.
The question paper will be in English language only.

For JEE Advanced Chemistry Preparation the priority order of topics is as follows:

  1. Atomic Structure.
  2. Periodic Table.
  3. Chemical Reaction.
  4. Stoichiometry.

How to Prepare for IIT JEE Advanced 2021 - Tips & Study Plan

Your stature in JEE Advanced Preparation rises when your approach towards the examination is solid, focused & directional.

JEE is most sought-after engineering entrance exam & Preparation for JEE Advanced can be incredibly hard when you’re missing some critical strategies needed to succeed.

Most of the Candidates are so engrossed in the preparation of JEE Mains Exam that they can’t see Advanced JEE coming until it hits the D-day.

To be honest, you can prepare for JEE Mains & Advanced altogether. But these two-tier exams are held separately for a reason.

While the aim of JEE Main Exam is to ensure that the candidate has complete knowledge of the concept of 11th & 12th PCM subjects, JEE Advanced is tougher. There is some difference in the syllabus of JEE Mains & Advanced Exam.

We all know (and wish were not true sometimes), mastering an engineering entrance exam doesn’t happen overnight.

Here are five golden rules for success in JEE Advanced:

JEE Advanced is an upgraded level of JEE Main. So, go ahead and dust off the Best Books for JEE Advanced. Sign up for the JEE Advanced preparation lessons and achieve great score.

Preparing your own notes is very important. Create notes after second or third reading of the chapter. When you have a collection of subject-wise notes all in one place. All in all, taking notes is a subtle, but powerful, way to make yourself more successful in learning & memorizing.

If you’re a visual thinker, try a mind map for preparing the notes. Start by writing the topic at the center of the page. From there, draw branches out to every key topic studied.

Without regular practice, the slip from Master to Journeyman is too easy. Practicing same concepts & topics again & again might be painful, but it preserves & build your preparation.

Prepare for Exam as per the latest JEE Advanced 2020 Pattern & Syllabus.

Think about it!

You solve 20 questions daily & you are content with your preparation level.

But, on the exam day, you’ll get two papers with 54 questions each.

And, you’ll have to solve the paper in 3 hours or less!

Get the Point!

If you set your preparation level optimal & practice a mock test that matches the difficulty level of the exam, you won’t be sweaty & anxious on the day of the Exam.

Better prepared than sorry...

How to Download JEE Advanced Admit Card? Hall Ticket to Advanced & AAT Exams

Your next step to appear in Joint Entrance Exam Advanced is downloading JEE Advanced Admit card. Do you remember, we asked you to save JEE Advanced Candidate Login details in earlier sections?

Here’s how you can download JEE Advanced Admit Card with your JEE Advanced Login:

  1. Visit JEE Advanced Candidate Portal. https://www.jeeadv.ac.in/
  2. Click on the JEE Advanced Admit card download link.
  3. Enter the required details like registration ID, DOB, Email ID & Mobile No.
  4. Click on Submit.
  5. JEE Advanced Hall Ticket will be displayed on the screen. Check your details & take a print out.

Note: Hall ticket for JEE AAT are not issued separately. JEE Advanced Admit card 2020 will be the valid document to get the entry in Architecture Aptitude Test Exam Hall. You can download the Admit Cards from the date of issuing the hall tickets to the IIT JEE Advanced 2021 Exam Date.

Advanced JEE Authority issues the Admit Card for successfully registered candidates. Those who don’t fulfill the eligibility criteria might face rejection of the Application or they might not be allowed to enter their respective JEE Advanced 2021 Exam Centers. Check your JEE Advanced Application Status to know about the disqualification.
